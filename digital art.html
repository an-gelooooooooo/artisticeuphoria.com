Digital Art

Digital art is the practice of creating and presenting art using technology. It is also known as computer art or multi-media art. It’s dependent on computer-based digital encoding and the electronic storage and processing of information in different formats; images, sounds. Computers today have become more powerful and portable, allowing more access to the digital world, enabling the industry to grow to a whole new level.

Everything we consume electronically today stands on the backbone of digital art. From the TV shows we watch, advertisements, software, applications, websites and billboards. The survival of all companies’ marketing is dependent on their use of visual art. To get a deeper understanding, let’s have a look at how it all began.

The History of Digital Art Who invented digital art? There are a few theories.

The first ever digital artwork piece dates back to 1952, created by Ben Laposky using an oscilloscope to manipulate electronic waves which appeared on a small fluorescent screen. The piece was known as Oscillon 40. One of the many unknown digital art facts is that the 1960s is said to be the era in which digital art came to existence, owing to John Whitney’s creation of the world’s first ever piece of art on a computer.

The term digital art was first used in 1980. This is the era when digital art became popular, thanks to the introduction of personal computers. Computer engineers at the time came up with a paint program known as AARON, which was used by the pioneering digital artist, Harold Cohen, designed to make large drawings on paper sheets placed on the floor. This example of early computer art changed the industry forever. To get a better understanding of the history and development of digital art, let’s explore its evolution here!
